# The 5 Hook Types

Reference for the hook-generator skill and any other skill that produces hooks.

## 1. Ask a question

**Mechanic:** Opens a curiosity gap by making the viewer mentally try to answer the question. Their brain commits to figuring out the answer, so they keep watching to see if they got it right.

**Why it works:** The brain is wired to fill in blanks. A question creates a blank. The viewer's own thought process becomes the hook.

**Examples:**
- "Should athletes eat pasta or rice before their game?"
- "When was the last time you did something that actually scared you?"
- "Why does your team always agree with you in meetings?"
- "What if motivation isn't the reason you can't stick to your gym routine?"

**Bad version:** "How do you grow on Instagram?" (too generic, viewer's brain doesn't bother answering)

**Good version:** "How can a video be posted twice but one receive 6 million more views?" (specific, surprising, brain wants to know)

## 2. Numbered list

**Mechanic:** Numbers feel structured and trackable. The brain wants to know what's on the list, especially the first item.

**Why it works:** Combines the curiosity gap (what's #1?) with a promise of digestibility (it's only X items). Viewer knows the cost of staying.

**Examples:**
- "Here are my top 5 most useful subreddits, from someone who hit financial freedom in her twenties."
- "3 reasons you're still not seeing results after months of training."
- "5 behaviours I changed before I ever stepped foot in the gym."

**Bad version:** "5 ways to grow on social media." (generic, no credibility hook, no specific identity)

**Good version:** "You'll blow up once you truly understand human behaviour." (combines list promise with contrarian payoff)

## 3. Tell a story

**Mechanic:** The brain refuses to abandon a story before resolution. Once you set up stakes, the viewer is mentally committed to seeing the ending.

**Why it works:** Narrative is the oldest cognitive grip humans have. Stories are remembered better than facts. They're almost involuntary to follow.

**Examples:**
- "I had a C-section four weeks ago, and the number of people I know who have had one and acted like it's no big deal is one million."
- "I was sitting in my car completely broke, wondering how it all went wrong so fast."
- "I worked out for months and still didn't get results."
- "I never got consistent with the gym until I made this small tweak to my day."

**Bad version:** "I was in the shower last night and this thought came to me, and then I..." (too long to get to the point, no stakes)

**Good version:** "A lot of the smartest people I know are the ones failing on social media." (immediate stakes, immediate curiosity, fast)

## 4. Negative hook

**Mechanic:** Loss aversion. Fear of missing the right thing or doing the wrong thing is twice as motivating as the desire to do the right thing.

**Why it works:** Evolutionary wiring. Avoiding danger has always been more urgent than seeking pleasure. "Don't", "stop", "never" hooks weaponise that.

**Examples:**
- "Never post on Instagram again without turning on these three settings."
- "The Stoics say you need to stop doing these five things."
- "You will never get the results you want if you keep going to the gym without a plan."
- "These are the worst products I've ever tried."

**Bad version:** "You shouldn't be inconsistent." (obvious, no urgency, no specifics)

**Good version:** "Never post on Instagram again without turning on these three settings." (specific, urgent, gives stakes)

## 5. Contrarian view

**Mechanic:** Take a widely held belief and flip it. The viewer must agree or disagree, and either way they keep watching to find out who's right.

**Why it works:** Cognitive dissonance is intolerable. The brain cannot resolve "this is true" and "this person is saying the opposite" without hearing the argument.

**Examples:**
- "Stop following your passion, it's keeping you broke."
- "You need to be playing dumb at work, like stupid."
- "Your workout routine doesn't matter."
- "We've been taught that being soft is weak, but actually it's a prerequisite to being strong."

**Bad version:** "Money isn't everything." (so widely accepted it isn't actually contrarian, no friction)

**Good version:** "Everyone is talking about FU money, but no one is talking about how real wealth is love-you money." (genuinely flips a default, sets up its own argument)
