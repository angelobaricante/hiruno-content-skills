# Doctor vs Patient Examples

Before/after pairs across different verticals. Use these as teaching examples and as a calibration set when rewriting.

## Health and fitness

| Doctor (expert-talk) | Patient (symptom-first) |
|---|---|
| "Five evidence-based interventions for circadian rhythm optimisation" | "Why you feel exhausted by 2pm even when you slept eight hours" |
| "Optimal macronutrient ratios for hypertrophy" | "Why your gym sessions aren't building the muscle you thought they would" |
| "Three corrective exercises for patellofemoral pain syndrome" | "If your knee gives out 20 minutes into a run, this is what's actually happening" |
| "Best practices for post-workout recovery" | "Why you're sore for three days after every leg day, and the rest of your week is wrecked" |
| "Strategies for sustainable weight loss" | "I lost 50 pounds, then gained 10 back, and it put me in a spiral" |

## How to use these

1. Calibration. Read through the column you're rewriting in (e.g. health and fitness if rewriting a fitness brand) before generating new symptom-first lines. It tunes your ear.
2. Direct theft. If a brand's vertical matches one of these closely, you can adapt the pattern directly. Just swap the specifics for the brand's audience symptoms.
3. Teaching. When the user asks "why doesn't this work?", show them the doctor version of their line, then the patient version, side by side.
